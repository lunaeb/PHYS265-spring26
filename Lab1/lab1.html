The gravitational potential at a distance $r$ from a mass $M$ is given by:

$$\Phi(r) = -\frac{GM}{r}$$

def grav(mass,xm,ym,x,y):
    """
    finds the gravitational potential energy at a point x,y 

    args: 
    "mass": the mass of a body, float
    "xm": the location of the mass (x), float
    "ym": the location of the mass (y), float 
    "x,y": location of a distant point to evaluate, float

    returns: 
    float: gravity at the point (x,y)
    """
    if not isinstance(mass or xm or ym or x or y, float):
        raise TypeError(f"input is of the wrong type")
    elif mass <= 0:
        raise ValueError(f"error: values must be positive")
    else: 
        xdist = np.abs(xm-x)
        ydist=np.abs(ym-y)
        r = np.sqrt(xdist**2 + ydist**2)
        gpe = -1.0 * (G * mass)/r 
        assert isinstance(gpe,float), "gravity isnt a float"
        assert gpe < 0, "gravity must be negative"
        return gpe

Part 3: The Gravitational Force Field of the Earth-Moon System¶

The gravitational force $\vec{F}$ that a mass $M_1$ exerts on a mass $m_2$ is:

$$\vec{F}_{21} = -G \frac{M_1 m_2}{|\vec{r}_{21}|^2} \hat{r}_{21}$$

where $\vec{r}_{21}$ is the displacement vector from $M_1$ to $m_2$.

  1. Code up a function that takes as an input the mass of a body $M_1$, the mass of a secondary body $m_2$, the cartesian location of the first mass $(x_1, y_1)$, and the cartesian location of the second body $(x_2, y_2)$. Have the function return the two gravitational force components, $F_x$ and $F_y$, experienced by the mass $m_2$ from the force exerted by $M_1$. Note that the gravitational force contains a singularity, and so if you made the approximation that the Earth is a finite point, you will need to avoid it. Like for the 1D potential, make sure it follows modern best-coding practices.

def force(mass1,mass2,x1,y1,x2,y2): 
    """
    finds the force that one mass exerts on another 

    args: 
    "mass 1,2": the mass of bodies 1 and 2, float
    "x 1,2": the location of the masses 1 and 2 (x), float
    "y 1,2": the location of the massses 1 and 2 (y), float 

    returns: 
    float: the gravitational force experienced by body 2
    """
    if not isinstance(mass1 or mass2 or x1 or y1 or x2 or y2, float):
        raise TypeError(f"input is of the wrong type")
    elif (mass1 or mass2) <= 0:
        raise ValueError(f"error: mass must be positive")
    else: 
        r_sqr=np.hypot(x1-x2,y1-y2)**2
        vec_r=np.array([x1-x2,y1-y2]) 
        vec_r_hat=vec_r/np.linalg.norm(vec_r)
        Fx = -1.0 * (G * mass1 * mass2) / r_sqr * vec_r_hat[0]
        Fy = -1.0 * (G * mass1 * mass2) / r_sqr * vec_r_hat[1]
        assert isinstance(Fx,float), "force isnt a float"
        assert isinstance(Fy,float), "force isnt a float"
        return Fx,Fy

\begin{equation} |\vec{F}_{moon}| = |\vec{F}_{earth}| \end{equation}

\begin{equation} G \frac{M_{moon} m}{|(x_{moon}-x)|} \frac{(x_{moon}-x)}{\sqrt{(x_{moon}-x)^2}} = G \frac{M_{earth} m}{|(x_{earth}-x)|} \frac{(x_{earth}-x)}{\sqrt{(x_{earth}-x)^2}} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{M_{moon}}{|(x_{moon}-x)|} = \frac{M_{earth}}{|(x_{earth}-x)|} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{(x_{moon}-x)}{M_{moon}} = \frac{(x_{earth}-x)}{M_{earth}} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{x_{moon}}{M_{moon}}-\frac{x}{M_{moon}} = \frac{x_{earth}}{M_{earth}}-\frac{x}{M_{earth}} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{x_{moon}}{M_{moon}}-\frac{x_{earth}}{M_{earth}} = -\frac{x}{M_{earth}}+\frac{x}{M_{moon}} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} \frac{x_{moon}}{M_{moon}}-\frac{x_{earth}}{M_{earth}} = -x\frac{1}{M_{earth}}+\frac{1}{M_{moon}} \end{equation}

\begin{equation} -\frac{x_{moon}}{M_{moon}}+\frac{x_{earth}}{M_{earth}}*({M_{earth}}+{M_{moon}}) = x \end{equation}

\begin{equation} -\frac{x_{moon}({M_{earth}}+{M_{moon}})}{M_{moon}}+\frac{x_{earth}({M_{earth}}+{M_{moon}})}{M_{earth}} = x \end{equation}

\begin{equation} x = \frac{x_{moon}M_{moon} + x_{earth}M_{earth}}{M_{earth}+M_{moon}}, y = \frac{y_{moon}M_{moon} + y_{earth}M_{earth}}{M_{earth}+M_{moon}} \end{equation}

Part 4: Altitude of the Saturn V Rocket¶

Rockets function by conservation of momentum. The ejection of fuel backwards propels the rocket forwards. The change in a rocket's velocity $\Delta v$ as a function of time $t$ (under a set of simplifying assumptions) is given by the Tsiolkovsky rocket equation:

$$\Delta v(t) = v_e \ln \left(\frac{m_0}{m(t)}\right) - gt$$

where $m_0$ is the initial "wet" mass (fuel + rocket parts + payload), $m(t) = m_0 - \dot{m}t$ is the mass at time $t$, $\dot{m}$ is the fuel burn rate (which we will assume to be constant), $v_e$ is the fuel exhaust velocity, and $g$ is the gravitational acceleration.

You can find the altitude $h$ of the rocket at "burnout" (when all the fuel is used up) by integrating the velocity over time:

$$h = \int_{0}^{T} \Delta v(t) \, dt$$

where $T$ is the total burn time of the rocket, which by conservation of momentum arguments is:

$$T = \frac{m_0 - m_f}{\dot{m}}$$

where $m_f$ is the final "dry mass" of the rocket, once all the fuel is burned.

def deltav(time,wetmass,drymass,burnrate,exhaust,gravity):
    """
    finds the change in velocity at a certain time 

    args: 
    "time": the tieme, float
    "wetmass": initial rocket mass, float
    "drymass": final rocket mass, float 
    "burnrate": rate at which the rocket burns fuel, float
    "exhaust": fuel exhaust velocity, float
    "gravity": gravitational acceleration, float

    returns: 
    float: change in velocity at the given time(s)
    """
    if not isinstance(wetmass or drymass or burnrate or exhaust, float):
        raise TypeError(f"input is of the wrong type")
    elif (time or wetmass or drymass) < 0:
        raise ValueError(f"error: values must be positive")
    else: 
        mt = wetmass - burnrate*time
        if mt <=0: 
            dvt=0
        else: 
            dvt = exhaust * np.log(wetmass/mt) - gravity*time
            assert isinstance(dvt,float), "delta v isnt a float"
        return dvt
